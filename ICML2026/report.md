# Papers on AI Reasoning, Vision–Language Models, Memory, and Related Topics

## Overview

The list below contains 28 recent papers (mostly from 2025–2026) covering a wide spectrum of topics such as theory formation, hallucination detection, causal reasoning, vision–language–action (VLA) models, reinforcement learning (RL), retrieval augmentation, human–robot collaboration, memory traces and data synthesis.  Each paper is given its full title (filling in truncated names where needed), a concise description based on its abstract, and an appropriate category.

### Key categories

- **World & theory modelling** – explicit reasoning frameworks that infer theories from observations.
- **Trustworthiness & hallucination detection** – techniques to detect hallucinations or provide reliability measures for models.
- **Benchmarks & evaluation** – new benchmarks for evaluating reasoning, causal thinking or navigation abilities.
- **Spatial & 3D reasoning** – methods or datasets enabling physical/spatial reasoning and 3D understanding.
- **Efficient reasoning & self‑evolving agents** – architectures for parallel reasoning or agents that improve themselves.
- **Causal reasoning & causal structure discovery** – benchmarks or algorithms for causal inference and discovery.
- **Vision‑language‑action (VLA) models** – research improving representation, supervision and behaviour in VLA systems.
- **Alignment, reinforcement learning & human‑robot collaboration** – methods for aligning models without explicit rewards, RL preference learning, understanding RL failures and stabilising multi‑agent cooperation.
- **Security & jailbreak attacks** – adversarial attacks and defences for image‑editing models.
- **Retrieval & RAG** – time‑aware retrieval systems for specialised domains.
- **Multimodal optimisation, memory & data synthesis** – nested optimisation across modalities, memory trace localisation in networks and data‑diversity synthesis.
- **Diffusion language models** – RL strategies for sampling in diffusion LMs.

## Comments on categorisation

The categories used above reflect broad research themes across these papers. Some papers naturally span multiple themes: for example, **HALO** deals with reinforcement learning and human–robot collaboration, while **Motive** (motion attribution) relates to both video generation and spatial reasoning.  For truncated entries where detailed abstracts were unavailable (such as **Multimodal Nested Learning for Decoupled and Coordinated Optimization**), the categorisation is provisional and based on the title and context. These categories should serve as a guide for further reading and exploration.
